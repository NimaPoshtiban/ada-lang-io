Readability

Express intent with explicitness and keywords over symbols and special structures

Express concepts like meaning in integers. Use built-in design by contract with pre/post-conditions and invariants. Model problems with typechecks and range constraints.
Separate specification and bodies
Types and functions, and their properties and documentation, are separated from their implementation. Control which parts are visible and to whom.
Ranges and precision
Specify a valid range of a discrete type or the required precision for floating-point and fixed-point types.
Predicates and contracts
Add predicates in the form of boolean expressions to your types and pre/post-conditions to your functions.
Representation clauses
Separate the high-level specification of types and their properties from the representation at the bit level.
Correctness

Build with technology used in 40 years of reliability in planes, trains, and satellites

Use the SPARK subset to formally verify part or all of your program, and integrate existing SPARK crates available in the Alire package manager.

SPARK 2014

From memory safety to functional correctness. One level at a time

Gradually adopt the SPARK subset to reach various levels of assurance. Higher levels take more effort, but give more benefits and stronger guarantees.
Stone - Valid SPARK
Restrict Ada packages to the SPARK subset. Avoids side-effects in functions and parameter aliasing.
Bronze - Initialization and correct data flow
No uninitialized variables are read or undesired access to globals occurs.
Silver - Absence of run-time errors
No buffer and arithmetic overflow, division by zero, or values out of range, among others, can occur.
Gold - Key integrity properties
Verify integrity of data and valid state transitions.
Platinum - Functional correctness
Full proof of functional correctness.
